I make mistakes.

  • It's hard to admit that, etc…wanting to be held up as an expert
  • I want you to think of me as perfect.

Thesis

We work in an environment in which perfection is a goal. Because of this, it is difficult to openly admit that we make mistakes. However, mistakes in software are inevitable. We must work to encourage a culture of openness so that we can learn from each other's mistakes and minimize feelings of isolation and shame.

  • Perfection: no build breaks, zero downtime, 100% code coverage
  • Inevitable. including community leaders. Come back to that

Blameless Postmortems

  • What actions they took at what time
  • What effects they observed
  • Expectations they had
  • assumptions they had made
  • Their understanding of timeline of events as they occurred

Change the conversation.

  • Change the conversation from thinking "I'm bad" to "I had a flaw in my thinking"
  • That's something you can learn from

Navigating Cancer

  • We've been thinking about how to work with production mistakes.
  • We've experimented with the following strategies.

Encourage engineering team communication

  • Dev days or half days
  • Teamwide postmortems
  • Lunch & Learn
  • Consultancy? Write a post
  • Dev days: People need to be encouraged to experiment. In order to foster creativity.
  • Postmortems: Production bugs are everyone's responsibility. Encourage openness and team wide communication. No blaming. Expose flaws in thinking.
  • Lunch & Learn Have a developer bring in a piece of broken code, a piece of difficult or problematic code, a side project, or whatever. Brainstorm ideas as a team. Encourage trust. How to think as a team.
  • Post: Not necessary to divulge all the details, but a lot of the googling I do that goes past StackOverflow is a consultancy that wrote a post on the topic. Position themselves as problem-solvers and experts.
