REM Sleep

Non-REM Sleep

  • During REM sleep, brain is active, stimulated (where dreams come from)
  • nREM is recovery; this is where we need to be restored

[The prefrontal cortex] plays a prominent role in waking executive functions which are preferentially impaired following sleep deprivation.

(Nofzinger, E., 2013, p. 203, my italics).
  • Executive tasks impaired following sleep deprivation, and restored after 'recovery sleep.'
  • Tasks involving executive function are adversely affected by, and thus especially vulnerable to, sleepiness
  • When working, especially when pairing, our prefrontal cortex is the first thing to go. Focused work is harder, productivity is scarcer
